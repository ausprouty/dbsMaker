Deepavali and the Light of Lord Jesus

Deepavali is a time when homes and hearts are filled with light. It is a season of reflection — on hope, renewal, and the longing for what brings peace within.

Many people find that this search for light is also a spiritual one. In the teachings of Lord Jesus, light is often used to speak about life, truth, and freedom from what weighs us down.

Lord Jesus said:

“I am the light of the world. Whoever follows me will not walk in darkness but will have the light of life.”

Lamps lit during Deepavali remind us that light is not only something we see, but something we seek. In the life of Lord Jesus, many have found a light that speaks to inner burdens — fear, guilt, restlessness, and longing for peace.

“In him was life, and that life was the light of all people. The light shines in the darkness, and the darkness has not overcome it.”

In many traditions, the present age is understood as a time of confusion and struggle. The teachings of Lord Jesus speak of hope beyond this — of a future shaped by peace, restoration, and enduring light.

Lord Jesus also invites those who feel weighed down to come to him — not with demands, but with honesty:

“Come to me, all you who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest. Take my yoke upon you and learn from me, for I am gentle and humble in heart, and you will find rest for your souls. For my yoke is easy, and my burden is light.”

During Deepavali, many pause to reflect on the light they are seeking. If you wish, you can take a moment to reflect or pray using the words below.

A prayer you may wish to pray

Lord Jesus, you have spoken of light that brings life and peace. If you are near, help me to see clearly. Lift the burdens I carry within, guide me in the way of peace, and lead me toward the light that brings rest to the soul.
